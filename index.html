Strategic defense

Meteor Madness applies NASA asteroid data (Sentry, SBDB, CAD, NeoWs) to simulate impact scenarios. A Monte Carlo model estimates entry energy, footprint, and hazards with uncertainty bands. Outputs are interactive maps and timelines that communicate planetary defense risks and mitigation options.

Impact simulation

Meteor Madness impact simulation uses NASA orbital and physical data to model entry angle, speed, and size. The engine estimates energy release, blast radius, thermal flux, and damage footprint with uncertainty bands. Results display on maps and timelines to show potential local and global effects.

About the game

Experience the ultimate planetary defense simulation where real science meets strategic gameplay. Defend Earth from asteroid threats using authentic NASA data and cutting-edge physics simulations.

The Story

In the year 2045, Earth's space agencies have detected an unprecedented number of Near-Earth Objects (NEOs) on collision courses with our planet. As the newly appointed Director of Planetary Defense, you must coordinate global efforts to protect humanity. Using real-time data from NASA's Center for Near Earth Object Studies (CNEOS), you'll track incoming threats, allocate resources, and deploy defensive systems ranging from kinetic impactors to gravity tractors. Every decision matters. Every asteroid deflected saves millions of lives. The fate of Earth rests in your strategic planning and scientific understanding.

Meteors

Meteors are pieces of rock or metal from space. When they fall toward Earth, they burn in the atmosphere and create bright streaks of light. Most meteors are small and disappear before reaching the ground, but some survive and become meteorites. Scientists study meteorites to learn about the formation of planets and the history of our solar system. Meteor showers happen when Earth travels through streams of space dust left by comets, creating many meteors in the sky at once.
